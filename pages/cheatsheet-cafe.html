💡 Why Vietnamese coffee is so strong: Vietnamese coffee uses robusta beans — higher caffeine, more bitter, and more intense than arabica. The condensed milk was historically used because fresh milk was scarce. The combination became iconic.

💡 "Không đá, không đường" (no ice, no sugar) is the health-conscious order. You'll hear locals say it constantly. It's completely normal and expected — Vietnamese cafés are used to customization requests.

💡 Trà sen (lotus tea) is made by stuffing green tea leaves inside lotus flowers overnight to absorb the scent. It's one of Vietnam's most prized and expensive teas — a beautiful gift if you find quality ones.

💡 Vietnamese café etiquette: Many cafés don't have a counter — find a seat first, then a server will come to you. Waving your hand gently or saying "Em ơi!" (younger sibling — for young staff) or "Anh/Chị ơi!" gets attention politely.
